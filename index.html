Why Use Projectivity?

Contribute to projects in a greater way

Entrepreneurs, Project Owners, Investors, and Donors are able to get tasks completed by subcontractors at discounted rates by committing to paying RWARDS based on the specific project’s success. You receive higher quality work and a better chance of overarching success or goal completion. You’re also able to form long-term relationships with freelancers ensuring that you find the right fit for your needs.

Contractors and Freelancers are able to contribute to projects in a greater way, see the value of the work you complete (with no fees or percentage removed), and become invested in your client’s success, which will enable you to be paid additional RWARDS when they meet their goals.

What is a Projectivity Reward?

Facilitate Growth

RWARD It is a payment obligation from the project owner/investor to contractors based on the success of the project. If the project meets its goals, everyone wins, and payments for rewards are distributed. These Rewards ensure projects’ success by uniting a team of contractors or freelancers to a common goal.

Micro-Task Payments with 0% Transaction Fees

It is now easier than ever to make micropayments across multiple countries on a per-task basis. Plus, with O% transaction fees, you are able to eliminate expensive freelancing platforms that charge both you and the contractor upwards of 20% per task. This allows you to cut your upfront costs for each and every task you outsource and still pay your contractors more than they would have received on those other platforms. The project manager gets better quality work at a lower cost, and the contractor gets paid more. This is the true definition of a “Win-

Win” situation.

Rewards Payments

Rewards payments take the traditional ESOP (Employee Stock Option Plan), which incentivizes traditional employees to perform at their highest level as they have financial incentive to see the company succeed and re-imagines this for the gig economy. Think of it like an EROP (Employee Reward Option Program) but instead of giving your contractor an actual stake in your company, you are able to commit rewards based on the value that specific tasks contribute to the success of a project. The more valuable the task, the higher you can make the reward, which results in contractors that are more “invested” in the success of the project
